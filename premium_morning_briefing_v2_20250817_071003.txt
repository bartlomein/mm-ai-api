Top stories:

A major product recall is underway. Empower Brands is recalling over 56,000 Remington hair dryers due to electrocution risks if dropped in water, according to the Consumer Product Safety Commission. The dryers lack an immersion protection device. Consumers are urged to unplug and stop using the product immediately and can submit a photo of the cut cord for a refund. The dryers were sold at Amazon, Target Marketplace, and Walmart Marketplace between March 2024 and June 2025 for twenty-five to thirty-two dollars.

DermaRite Industries is issuing a nationwide recall of its soaps because of potential bacterial contamination. The FDA announced that the soaps, sold in New York State and across the country, may cause serious and life-threatening infections, including sepsis. The recall involves over a dozen skin-cleansing soaps, foams, and lotions including DermaKleen lotion soaps and KleenFoam soap. According to the FDA, several lots were found to be contaminated with Burkholderia cepacia, a microbe that can easily spread and is difficult to treat, especially in immunocompromised individuals.

European leaders are set to join Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky when he meets with U.S. President Donald Trump at the White House on Monday for peace talks. Ursula von der Leyen, Friedrich Merz, and Alexander Stubb have confirmed their attendance. This follows a coalition of the willing video call on Sunday afternoon, hosted by Sir Keir Starmer, Emmanuel Macron, and Friedrich Merz. Reports indicate President Trump may urge President Zelensky to cede control of Donetsk and Luhansk to Russia in exchange for ending the war.

Nine people have died and three are missing after flash floods swept through a wild camping site in Inner Mongolia, China. State-run media reports the disaster occurred on Saturday morning. Over 700 rescuers are searching for the missing. The Ministry of Emergency Management has warned travelers to avoid undeveloped wilderness areas during flood season.

The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation Trust reduced its stake in Microsoft in the second quarter of 2025, according to its 13F filing with the United States Securities and Exchange Commission. At the same time, the trust increased its investment in Berkshire Hathaway by forty point four eight percent, raising its value to eleven point seven two billion dollars. Microsoft now makes up twenty-seven point two seven percent of its portfolio and is valued at thirteen point zero three billion dollars.

Experts anticipate the launch of a Solana Exchange Traded Fund, ticker S-O-L E-T-F --, by the end of 2025. According to The Block CEO of Two Prime Digital Assets, Alexander Blum, the United States' crypto-friendly policies and the success of Bitcoin and Ethereum-based exchange-traded funds suggest spot Solana ETFs may emerge. Canadians experienced air travel disruptions as Air Canada flight attendants went on strike. The Canadian government intervened, forcing the airline and striking flight attendants back to work and into arbitration. According to Air Canada, flights are planned to resume on Sunday evening, but it will take several days before operations return to normal, with some flights being canceled over the next seven to ten days. The strike stranded more than 100,000 travelers.

Global developments:

Pakistan is grappling with severe flooding due to monsoon rains. More than 200 people are missing in one district, according to an official. A provincial rescue spokesman told news agency AFP that "ten to twelve entire villages" were partially buried. At least 650 people have been killed so far this year. Government forecasters say heavy rainfall is expected until August twenty-first in the north-west, where several areas have been declared disaster zones.

Southern African leaders are meeting in Madagascar for the Southern African Development Community summit this Sunday and Monday. The region's mining industry is the main topic on the agenda, and they're also due to discuss the ongoing crisis in Eastern Democratic Republic of Congo, according to France 24.

According to reports, Indonesia will begin airdropping aid into Gaza Strip as the IDF expands operations. KAN News reports that the request specifically came from within the political echelon. Indonesia will be one of eleven countries executing aid drops in Gaza.

South Korea’s move to lower tensions with Kim Jong Un is threatening an estimated 400,000 Christians in North Korea. President Lee Jae-myung ordered a reported eighty percent of radio broadcasts from the South beamed into the North to stop transmitting in May. According to Fox News, the policy has been slammed as "a catastrophic strategic mistake."
