Top stories.

European leaders, including leaders from France, Britain and Germany will join Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy in Washington, DC on Monday for talks with United States President Donald Trump on ending Russia's war in Ukraine. This comes after Trump's summit with Russian President Vladimir Putin in Alaska, from which Zelenskyy was excluded. Special US envoy Steve Witkoff said Putin agreed to allow the US and European allies to offer Ukraine a security guarantee resembling NATO's collective defense mandate as part of an eventual deal to end the three and a half year war. European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen welcomed Trump's willingness to contribute to Article 5-like security guarantees for Ukraine.

In Pakistan, weather authorities warned of heavier torrential rains as the death toll from flash floods hit three hundred twenty seven. Nearly six hundred fifty people have died due to these rains since their beginning in June. The weather agency issued a heavy rain alert countrywide from August seventeenth to August twenty first, recommending people in the North-Western region of the country to take precautionary measures. The National Disaster Management Authority also warned the people of the nation about downpours, which began earlier than usual this year.

In Kashmir, a sudden, intense downpour triggered deadly flooding in the Kishtwar district, killing at least forty six people and leaving more than two hundred missing. The incident struck Chasoti town, a key stop on the pilgrimage route to the Machail Mata temple, when floodwaters swept away a community kitchen and a security post set up for pilgrims. The Indian Meteorological Department defines a cloudburst as a sudden downpour of over one hundred millimeters of rain in one hour.

The flight attendants went on strike over wages and are seeking to be paid for work they perform on the ground before takeoff and after landing.

Now for global developments.

A shooting at a restaurant called "Taste Of The City Lounge" in the Crown Heights neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York City, left three people dead and eight others injured. According to NBC News, New York Police Department Commissioner Jessica Tisch stated at a press conference that there were multiple attackers involved in the incident, and she mentioned that the investigation is ongoing and no one has been arrested yet.

German wildfire specialist units from Bonn, Dusseldorf and other cities have been deployed to Spain to help combat severe wildfires. The units specialize in wildfire suppression and are being accompanied by firefighters from Lower Saxony. Spain requested the assistance from the EU Civil Protection Mechanism.

Hundreds of people participated in an event marking India Day in Dublin, Ireland, as authorities sought to assure the community that action was being taken against racially motivated crime. The celebration was organized for India's seventy ninth independence day and featured music, dance, food stalls, and cultural activities. Minister for Justice Jim O'Callaghan expressed concern over unacceptable attacks on the Indian community, adding that the majority appear to have been carried out by young people.
