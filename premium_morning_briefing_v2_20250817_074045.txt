Top stories...

European leaders, including European Commission president Ursula von der Leyen, German chancellor Friedrich Merz, and French president Emmanuel Macron, are set to join Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky when he meets with US President Donald Trump in Washington D.C. on Monday. The meeting follows Trump's summit with Russian President Vladimir Putin in Alaska, where discussions on the Russia-Ukraine conflict took place. Reports indicate Trump is considering urging Zelensky to accept a Russian land grab of Donetsk and Luhansk as a condition for ending the war, a proposal that European leaders may oppose.

Air Canada is planning to resume flights on Sunday after the Canadian Industrial Relations Board acted on a directive from the country's Jobs Minister Patty Hajdu to end a cabin crew strike. The strike had caused the suspension of around seven hundred daily flights, stranding over one hundred thousand travelers. The airline expects it will take seven to ten days for the schedule to stabilise and return to normal.

Foreign portfolio investors offloaded approximately twenty one thousand crore rupees from Indian equity holdings in early August, according to timesofindia dot indiatimes dot com. Analysts attribute this selloff to US-India trade disputes, muted first quarter corporate results, and rupee depreciation. The total FPI equity outflows reached one point sixteen lakh crore rupees in 2025, as per depository records.

A magnitude six point zero earthquake struck central Sulawesi, Indonesia, early on Sunday, injuring twenty nine people, according to the country's disaster mitigation agency BNPB. Two people were critically injured. There were no immediate reports of deaths.

Eli Lilly shares experienced a decline earlier this month after the company released its latest earnings numbers, according to The Motley Fool. Despite sales growth of forty one percent in the first six months of the year due to its GLP-1 drugs, underwhelming news about its weight loss pill, orforglipron, led to a selloff. While the pill helped people lose close to twelve percent of their weight in a late-stage trial, this fell short of the fifteen percent analysts anticipated.

Now for global developments...

Russia has proposed terms for ending the war in Ukraine following talks between President Donald Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin, according to GreekReporter dot com. The proposal involves Ukraine conceding full control of the Donbas region, including Donetsk and Luhansk, in exchange for Russia withdrawing troops from certain occupied zones. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky is scheduled to meet with Trump in Washington on Monday to discuss the proposal.

More than two hundred people are missing in the Buner district of Pakistan due to devastating monsoon flooding and landslides, according to bbc dot com. Flash floods have killed more than three hundred people in Pakistan and Pakistan-administered Kashmir in recent days. Government forecasters anticipate heavy rainfall until August twenty first in the north-west.

Royal Oman Police has arrested six people for possession of drugs, Khaleej times reports. The men possessed over thirty two kilograms of crystal meth and twenty three kilograms of marijuana. The arrested individuals were of Asian origin.

France calls on Israel to abandon the E1 colonial project, according to Ammon News. The French Foreign Ministry condemned the Israeli settlement plan in the E1 area, which would separate Jerusalem from the West Bank.
