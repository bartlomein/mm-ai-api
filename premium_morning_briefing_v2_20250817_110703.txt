Climate change-induced flash floods have killed at least 337 people in northwestern Pakistan. Al Jazeera reports that dozens remain missing after torrential rains and cloudbursts triggered deadly flooding. Residents have accused officials of failing to provide adequate warning to evacuate. Authorities have warned of more deluges and possible landslides between now and Tuesday.

A total of 67 personnel, with 21 vehicles and three trailers, have been deployed and are expected to arrive on Tuesday evening.

The commerce ministry’s investigation arm in India has recommended a three-year safeguard duty on imports of certain flat steel products. The recommendation followed a complaint by the Indian Steel Association.

An extension of the scheme means an extra two point seven million households will qualify this winter.
