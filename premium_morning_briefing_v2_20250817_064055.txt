Top stories:

Air Canada flights are set to resume today, according to apnews.com, after the Canadian government intervened to end a strike by flight attendants and forced the airline into arbitration. The strike had stranded more than 100,000 travelers. While flights are restarting, Air Canada anticipates that it will take several days for operations to return to normal, and some flights may be canceled over the next seven to ten days.

In Washington, D.C., tensions are escalating due to an increased presence of law enforcement, according to BayToday.ca. National Guard members are stationed near the Washington Monument after President Trump declared a crime emergency. Protests have occurred with people expressing outrage over the President's actions and holding signs with messages like "Hands off D.C." West Virginia, Ohio, and South Carolina have authorized additional National Guard members to go to Washington.

The planned visit by U.S. trade negotiators to New Delhi from August 25th to 29th has been called off, according to The Business Times. This delays talks on a proposed trade agreement and reduces the chances of relief from additional U.S. tariffs on Indian goods scheduled to begin on August 27th. The reason for the postponement has not been disclosed. Earlier this month, President Trump imposed an additional 25 percent tariff on Indian goods, citing New Delhi's continued imports of Russian oil.

According to multiple news sources, flash floods in China have caused devastation, leading to at least eight deaths. In Inner Mongolia, a river burst its banks, washing away thirteen campers. In Sichuan province, two people died and three were injured at a beer festival when a truss fell. The central government has allocated forty-four million pounds in fresh funding for disaster relief, bringing the total allocated since April to at least five hundred and ninety-six million pounds.

The federal funds rate is likely to remain the primary tool for responding to trends in employment and inflation.

In Pakistan, authorities defended their response to flash floods that killed more than 200 people in Buner, according to The Journal. Climate change is believed to be a factor in the increased rainfall.
